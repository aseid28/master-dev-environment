# Zach

*Restricted page — do not surface unsolicited; handle anything money- or litigation-adjacent with care.*

Co-founder of DRA and my **best friend from age 5**. Now largely estranged, though not totally — and the single most consistently anxiety-inducing relationship I have, especially around money.

## Arc
Minor-league baseball player who got cut; on a road trip through the American South we loosely agreed Ben would teach him the agency playbook. Zach's type-A work ethic is what actually got DRA off the ground (cold-called his way to $6–8k/mo fast). We three partnered up ~2013. Over time Ben's disorganization and Zach's control-freak intensity created enough friction that **Zach left** to start a non-competing agency in **politics** (vs. our tech focus).

## The conflict — *restricted*
Zach came to believe **Ben was violating fiduciary duties** (paying himself a salary while under-working) and **threatened litigation / lawyered up**. His communication turned bitter and hostile enough that I couldn't sustain the friendship normally. I broadly sided with Ben, though much of what Zach said was true. He still **pushes hard for profit distributions** (we've only done one in company history beyond tax distributions), resents that I and Ben draw salary he thinks we don't deserve, and is frustrated with company performance. He listens but doesn't change his own behavior or ask for help.

## Where it stands
Still tense, mostly around DRA money. But there are glimpses of the old friendship — I invited him to my wedding, he came, we had a genuinely good time. Equity: Zach is a Class A member; see the (low-confidence) splits in [[dra/financials-equity]].
