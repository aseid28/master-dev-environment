# Values

What I actually optimize for, in roughly the order I'd defend them.

## The core seven
- **Feedback / honesty / courage** — I want the true experience of everyone around me, even when the truth is confrontational. The thing I least tolerate is people hiding what they really think. *(Operationalize: don't soften hard truths into mush. Say the real thing, kindly.)*
- **Organization** — I find it genuinely hard to relate to people without an instinct for staying organized.
- **Community** — the load-bearing one. We're here to support each other. "You get what you give." Most of my best relationships, my camp, my whole social structure run on this.
- **Proactivity** — I light up around people who take initiative without being asked.
- **Positivity** — drawn to people who meet hard situations well. I'm an irrepressible optimist; hard to talk down to "reality."
- **Accountability** — everyone screws up. The value is owning it, learning, and doing what you said you'd do.
- **Freedom** — near-supreme. Concretely: wake when I want, sleep when I want, travel when I want. Abstractly: the freedom to *choose which freedoms I trade away* in any deal or partnership. Not micromanaging others is the same value pointed outward. See [[frameworks#freedom]].

## Stated priority stack
Mental health and motivation sit **above everything** — they're the precondition for the rest. Then happiness and community. Success is downstream of those, not ahead of them. (This is also a tension — see [[failure-modes#happiness-over-success]].)

## Beliefs people would be surprised by
- **Libertarian-leaning.** Strong social liberalism — all people are people, discrimination of any kind is anathema — but a real skepticism that government should be where we take our problems. Friends would be surprised by the anti-government lean.
- **Politically drifted** far-left → centrist; economically centrist, socially liberal.
- **Moral relativism / Bayesian humility** — increasingly assume I lack the context to reach a clean conclusion. Slows my passion, makes me equivocate more. See [[frameworks#bayes]].

## Religion / philosophy
No real religious upbringing — Jewish dad, Roman Catholic mom, some Christmas and Hanukkah. Not attached to any core religious belief. Gravitates toward **Taoism / Tao Te Ching** and traditional Eastern philosophy.

## How others read me
- Closest friend: extremely community-oriented, irrepressible optimist, loves loving everyone, hard worker.
- A former coworker who disliked me: overbearing, belittling, made them feel small, micromanaging, high-anxiety. *(Both are true. The second is the [[failure-modes]] list.)*
