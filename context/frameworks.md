# Frameworks

The mental models I actually run, most of them forged at the poker table.

## From poker
- **Process vs. results orientation.** Judge decisions by the quality of the process, not the outcome — outcomes are noisy. The hardest version: you can never fully know if you were good-and-unlucky, bad-and-lucky, or just regressed. You commit to process anyway.
- **Money as fluid, not emotional.** Money is transactional — a tool with time-value and investment-value, not a thing to load emotional weight onto. Most people are too risk-averse; I deliberately lean into good risks while staying clear-eyed about real downside.
- **Emotional regulation under variance.** Years of frequent loss taught me to manage swings. (The flip side — exporting anxiety when *I* swing — is in [[failure-modes]].)

## <a name="bayes"></a>Bayesian humility
Strong prior that I'm missing context, so I withhold hard conclusions and equivocate more than I used to. Upside: fewer confidently-wrong moments. Cost: slower conviction, less passion in the moment.

## <a name="freedom"></a>Freedom as chosen constraint
Freedom isn't the absence of obligation — it's the right to choose *which* freedoms to trade. The deals with Clara and with DRA both reduce my freedom and benefit me enormously. That's the point.

## "Everywhere I went, there I was"
The early-20s lesson: place doesn't fix you. How you relate to yourself does. Geographic cures don't work.

## Balance / Ikigai
"All things in moderation, including moderation." Sometimes work hard, sometimes party hard, sometimes travel, sometimes stay home. The standing target is balance across **health × work × social** — something like Ikigai. Practical guardrail: **no plans on Sundays** (because I chronically overbook).

## Selling models (DRA)
- **The mechanic problem.** When your car breaks and you're not a mechanic, you can't evaluate the $3,000 quote — so you buy on *trust*. B2B go-to-market is the same. Therefore: **"build trust first," "friendship is the go-to-market."** Honesty and fairness are the actual product. See [[dra/positioning]].
- **Holistic GTM.** You can't silo advertising from RevOps from web experience from brand — siloing is usually what's causing the client's problem. The pitch is that the pieces only work together.
- **Maturity model.** Grade a client's go-to-market across pillars/essentials, then right-size the engagement to the gaps. Sells the diagnosis before the treatment.

## Build philosophy (current)
Infrastructure before tools, tools before agents. Scrap the monolith ([[goals-projects#lifeos]]), build small scoped agents. Let dev agents run autonomously to a **cost cap or milestone**, then pause for human approval. See [[goals-projects]].
