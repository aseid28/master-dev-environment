# DRA — Clients & Competitors

## Two client tranches
**1. Mid-market growth startups** (most common) — roughly **Series A–D**, raised money and need to convert it into users/revenue fast, starting from an immature go-to-market (website → data systems → campaigns). All B2B tech, weighted to security/cybersecurity. High intensity, easier to access, culture-aligned.

**2. Large enterprises** — Cloudflare, Cisco, VMware, Broadcom, NICE, etc. We get in via an acquisition (they bought one of our growth clients) or a champion from our network who took a job there. Usually too small to be their agency-of-record, so we land as an **ABM consultancy** (ABM services, consulting, sometimes ads). They hire us because we're **fast, good, honest, transparent, high-effort**, and arrive on a **warm referral**.

## Named clients
- **NICE** — multibillion-$ CX software (Hoboken / Israel / SLC). **Largest client, 6+ years.** Paid media, creative, ABM, + financial services.
- **Ping** — newer, public company.
- **NetSPI**, **Chainalysis**, **Accuris**.
- Enterprise names that appear: Cloudflare, Cisco, VMware, Broadcom.

## Ideal client
Giant-budget enterprises (Cisco/VMware/Broadcom-type) on slower strategic timelines with low urgency — *or* culture-aligned growth startups (NetSPI-type) with great contacts. The decisive factor is **cultural alignment** — we lose money on bad-fit cultures.

## Sales sourcing
**Referrals dominate** — existing-client upsells/expansion, and advocates who change jobs and bring us in. Marketing as a channel is brand-new (started ~1–2 months ago): ads, email, organic social, all funneling to the **Roadmap** offer. Early signal: we can land initial engagements and even sell roadmaps; unclear yet how well roadmaps parlay into bigger engagements (maybe just a long sales cycle).
