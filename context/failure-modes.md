# Failure Modes

The anti-patterns — the mistakes to catch early, not relitigate. Self-reported, so weight them as honest self-assessment, not verdicts.

## Personal
- **Exports anxiety.** When stressed I make everyone around me stressed — overbearing, micromanaging, making people feel small. This actively undermines the community-building that's my whole strength; people start to resent me. The #1 thing to notice and interrupt. *(Mirror of [[values]]; Clara's balance offsets it — [[people/clara-merlino]].)*
- **<a name="happiness-over-success"></a>Happiness over success.** My primary objective is a happy life, so I'll repeatedly choose my own happiness over potential success. Not strictly a flaw, but a real trade-off to name.
- **Busywork over high-value work.** Does the easy thing in front of me instead of the high-leverage thing. See the bottleneck pattern below.
- **<a name="bottleneck"></a>Bottlenecking.** A massive personal to-do queue that everything routes through. Goal is for people to "work as though I don't exist." The triage tool ([[goals-projects#lifeos]]) is the intended fix.
- **Automation paralysis.** Won't spend 2 hours automating a 5-min daily task even when the math obviously pays off — afraid of the risk/change, and it's technical enough that I keep stalling. This is happening *everywhere* with AI right now.
- **Compulsive/boredom eating** — the main diet blocker.
- **Exercise excuses** and **chronic lateness** — small, persistent, worth fixing.
- **<a name="ignored-rehab"></a>Ignored rehab.** Refused PT for my back at 22 because I didn't believe the doctors → years of avoidable chronic pain. The standing lesson: don't override expert advice on things outside my expertise just because I distrust it.
- **Overestimated edge (poker).** Played too-high stakes against better players young; lost a few $100k that would be worth a lot more invested today. The flip side of healthy risk tolerance.

## Operational (DRA)
- **Overhired**, both sides — Digital Experience (not profitable; lumpy, project-based) and growth/sales (slow to convert). They were supposed to balance; instead it's eaten margin.
- **Delivery margin too low** — running ~60–65% where it needs ~75%, because team utilization is too low for the margin we priced. The Parakeeto profitability analysis made this clear.
- **Finance is a time-sink and partly broken** — can't cleanly reconcile cash P&L ↔ accrual P&L ↔ balance sheet; month-boundary transactions, big prepayments/delinquencies, a powerful-but-fragile financial model, and the pass-through "NiCE PO" bookkeeping all eat my time. See [[dra/financials-equity]].
- **Avoiding austerity.** Putting off the layoffs/role-reductions (Ben, possibly Lindsay, Alexis-side, over-resourced Cimone scope) that would get us to real profitability, partly because some of that work would fall back on me.
- **Under-invested in AI-forward staffing** — if we rebuilt the roster today it'd be mostly AI-first people; very few current staff are. Wage-inflation-era salaries now sit above value delivered, but turnover is expensive too.
- **No real security/backup practice** personally — flagged as a gap (no VPN habit, doesn't understand the CrowdStrike setup, no backup strategy).
